Why Relationships Get Stuck

During conflict, we often lose access to calm communication. The body moves into stress responses—the conversation is no longer about the topic, but about two nervous systems reacting to a perceived threat.

  • • Raised voice
  • • Emotional flooding
  • • Shutting down
  • • Criticism
  • • Withdrawal
  • • Defensiveness

What You Will Practice & Learn

1. Early Signs of Activation

Notice what happens in your body before conflict escalates (clenched jaw, shallow breathing, neck tension).

2. Communicating Feelings

Learn to express internal experience ("I feel unheard") instead of blame to reduce defensiveness.

3. Partner Cues

Notice signs of activation in your partner with respect, turning them into cues for care.

4. Real-Time Regulation

Simple tools for calming the nervous system through grounding and slowing communication.

5. Skillful Pausing

Learn how to pause a conversation without abandoning it, allowing for a reset.

6. Constructive Anger

Understand anger as a signal for unmet needs or boundaries without turning it into an attack.
